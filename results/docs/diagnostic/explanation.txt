diagnostic/docs/results_explanation.txt
========================================
Explanation of M2 SHAP diagnostic results (results.txt)
Date: 2026-05-25


OVERVIEW
--------
The diagnostic fitted M2 (causally-blind XGBoost on CB_ALL_FEATURES) on
the full 21,714-row CB training dataset and computed SHAP values to
attribute its predictive signal across feature groups. The headline
metrics confirm the near-perfect performance expected from the experiment:
R²(log) = 0.9991, MdAPE = 1.64%.

The SHAP attribution reveals the mechanism behind that performance — and
it is not what the script's own summary emphasises. The full picture is
more significant than originally anticipated.


WHAT THE RESULTS ACTUALLY SHOW
--------------------------------

Feature group breakdown:

  Mediators (electricity, gas, steam, chilled water, other fuel) : 57.2%
  Upstream causal (floor area, property type, year built, etc.)  : 26.1%
  Derived / circular (ghg_intensity, EUIs, energy star, etc.)    : 15.0%
  Compliance metadata                                            :  1.0%
  Spatial                                                        :  0.6%
  Water use                                                      :  0.0%

Top individual features:

  electricity_use_kbtu               42.3%   mediator
  gross_floor_area_buildings_sq_ft   20.3%   upstream causal
  natural_gas_use_kbtu               13.4%   mediator
  ghg_intensity_kg_co2e_sq_ft         9.3%   derived / circular
  data_year                           5.2%   upstream causal

The finding differs from the script's initial framing. The script
expected ghg_intensity to dominate as the primary circular feature.
Instead, electricity_use_kbtu accounts for 42.3% of total SHAP signal —
nearly three times the contribution of ghg_intensity.


WHY ELECTRICITY DOMINATES INSTEAD OF GHG INTENSITY
----------------------------------------------------
Total GHG emissions are computed by multiplying each fuel source by its
EPA emission factor:

  total_GHG ≈ electricity × EF_e + natural_gas × EF_g
            + district_steam × EF_s + ...

XGBoost can learn this relationship directly from the mediator columns
without needing to go through ghg_intensity (which is itself derived from
the same fuel data). The relationship electricity → GHG is a direct
monotone mapping. Given that electricity is the dominant energy source
in Chicago commercial buildings, it captures the single largest slice of
predictive signal.

ghg_intensity still contributes 9.3% — it provides a marginal advantage
by compressing the fuel-to-GHG transformation into one feature — but it
is not the primary vehicle for M2's exploitation. The mediators are.


THE STRONGER ARGUMENT
---------------------
The original paper framing emphasised circular derived features as the
mechanism behind M2's inflated performance. The SHAP results refine and
strengthen this argument:

  Mediators + derived/circular = 57.2% + 15.0% = 72.2% of total SHAP

72.2% of M2's predictive signal comes from features that are either:

  (a) Post-treatment (mediators) — only observed for compliant buildings
      that have already submitted a report. Non-compliant buildings have
      no actual mediator data at deployment; the model receives imputed
      training column medians for all mediator columns.

  (b) Circular / tautological (derived metrics) — computed from the same
      fuel data that produces GHG emissions. Including ghg_intensity as a
      predictor of total_GHG is definitionally circular.

Only 26.1% of M2's signal comes from upstream causal features — the only
features that are genuinely available and causally valid for any building,
compliant or not.


WHY THIS EXPLAINS THE DEPLOYMENT FAILURE
-----------------------------------------
When M2 is applied to the 5,510 non-compliant buildings (the deployment
test in the experiment), those buildings have no observed energy data.
Every mediator column and every derived metric column is imputed with the
training median — a constant value identical for every non-compliant
building.

The consequence: 72.2% of M2's signal collapses to a constant at
deployment. The model's predictions then depend almost entirely on the
26.1% upstream signal (floor area, property type, year built), producing
predictions equivalent to:

  GHG_deploy ≈ f(floor_area, property_type, year) + constant

where "constant" is the contribution from median-imputed mediators and
derived metrics — the same for every non-compliant building regardless of
its actual energy behaviour. This is why the deployment experiment found
M2's predictions centred on ~641 metric tons with variation driven only by
floor area — the imputed mediators provide no cross-building differentiation.

This is the more insidious failure mode noted in the experiment docs:
the predictions look numerically plausible (641 metric tons is within the
reasonable range), but the cross-building variation is causally wrong.
The model assigns the same "average compliant building" energy profile to
every non-compliant building irrespective of its actual characteristics.


REVISED PAPER ARGUMENT
-----------------------
The SHAP results support a refined version of the circular feature
exploitation argument:

  Original framing: M2 exploits ghg_intensity (a circular derived metric)
  to achieve near-perfect CV metrics.

  Refined framing: M2 exploits post-treatment mediator variables (57.2%
  of SHAP) and circular derived metrics (15.0% of SHAP) — together 72.2%
  of its predictive signal. These features are unavailable or causally
  invalid at deployment. The model is not learning the GHG data-generating
  process; it is learning to reconstruct the EPA emission accounting formula
  from the observed fuel inputs of buildings that have already reported.
  Causal XGB (M5), restricted to upstream features only, achieves 26.1%
  of M2's feature basis — and this is the honest performance ceiling.

This is a stronger argument because it shows the problem is not limited to
one circular feature (ghg_intensity) but is structural: any model trained on
mediators will face the same deployment failure for the same reason, and the
72.2% figure quantifies the scale of that structural unsoundness.


WHAT THIS DOES NOT CHANGE
--------------------------
These results do not alter the experiment's conclusions. The deployment test
already demonstrated M2's miscalibration on non-compliant buildings. The
SHAP analysis explains the mechanism: it is the mediator exploitation (not
just derived feature exploitation) that drives both the inflated CV metrics
and the deployment failure.

The pre-registered prediction P1 — "M2 CB XGB will achieve near-perfect CV
metrics via circular/tautological feature selection" — is still corroborated.
The SHAP results add precision: the near-tautological relationship operates
primarily through mediators (direct fuel → GHG mapping) rather than through
ghg_intensity alone.
