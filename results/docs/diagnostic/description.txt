diagnostic/
===========
Purpose
-------
This folder contains targeted diagnostic analyses that support and
strengthen specific claims made in the paper. Diagnostics are not
part of the main experiment (experiments/) — they do not introduce
new models or new comparisons. They take an existing model from the
experiment and probe it in depth to produce evidence for a specific
argument.

Each diagnostic is a self-contained script that reuses the exact
preprocessing and feature definitions from experiments/scripts/pipeline.py.
Results are saved to diagnostic/figures/.


Contents
--------
m2_shap.py
    SHAP attribution analysis for M2 (causally-blind XGBoost).

    Fits M2 on the full CB training dataset (~21,714 rows, CB_ALL_FEATURES)
    and computes SHAP values to quantify which features drive its near-perfect
    CV performance (R² > 0.99, MdAPE < 3%).

    Argument it supports: M2's apparent performance is an exploitation
    artefact, not genuine predictive skill. Post-treatment mediators —
    primarily electricity_use_kbtu (42.3% of total mean |SHAP|) and
    natural_gas_use_kbtu (13.4%) — account for the dominant share of
    M2's predictive signal, because they are direct inputs to the EPA
    GHG formula and XGBoost learns the near-identity mapping. Circular
    derived metrics (including ghg_intensity_kg_co2e_sq_ft, 9.3%)
    contribute additionally. Together these two groups account for 72.2%
    of M2's signal — features that are either causally post-treatment or
    algebraically derived from the target, and therefore unavailable or
    invalid at deployment. SHAP values make this breakdown quantified and
    visually demonstrable.

    Outputs: fig_shap_beeswarm.png, fig_shap_bar.png, fig_shap_group.png

    Run: uv run python diagnostic/m2_shap.py


figures/
    Output directory for all diagnostic figures. Figures are named
    fig_<diagnostic>_<content>.png and are referenced by the paper
    when supporting specific claims.


Relationship to the main experiment
-------------------------------------
The 2x3 factorial experiment (experiments/) establishes the comparison
between the causally-blind and causal pipelines and documents all
pre-registered predictions. Diagnostics sit one level below: they take
a result from the experiment (e.g., M2's near-perfect CV metrics) and
open it up to show the internal mechanism.

A diagnostic finding does not change the experiment results. It adds
mechanistic depth to an already-observed result.
