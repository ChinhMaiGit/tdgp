Reading:
 - Electricity and TOTAL metered energy: strongly area-driven, near-proportional
   (beta_area ~ 1.0; area alone explains ~44-47%). Clean corroboration of X_s -> M
   and of the proportional (log1p) scaling.
 - Natural gas: weak on area alone (R2=0.045), rises to 0.123 with property type and
   0.183 with all controls;
   gas use is system/type-dependent, not size-driven. Upstream still explains it
   modestly, with type (X_s) carrying most of it.
 - District steam / chilled water (structural zeros): area ~ irrelevant
   (beta ~ 0.03, 0.46) but property type + year explain presence well (R2_full
   0.77-0.81). The "link" here is X -> presence on the district network, not
   X(area) -> magnitude -- consistent with their structural-zero nature.
 - Association only; the X -> M direction rests on physical/temporal order
   (building characteristics precede and drive energy use), not these fits.

Reading:
 - Adjusted R^2 rises for every mediator, so data_year -> M is genuine, not a
   mechanical R^2 gain from more regressors. Effect is small for the dominant
   fuels (electricity / total ~1-1.5 pp), modest for gas (~5 pp), large for the
   district structural zeros (40-54 pp, most likely year-varying reporting /
   coverage of those niche fields rather than real consumption).
 - At n ~ 22k the partial-F p-values are all ~0 (everything is significant);
   the adjusted-R^2 delta is the honest effect-size read.
 - Framing: in the paper these regressions CORROBORATE pre-specified edges (from
   documents + physical/temporal logic), they are NOT variable selection. R^2
   stays the reported metric; the full-model adjusted R^2 is shown alongside in
   Table 4.3 only to demonstrate the parameter count is immaterial at this n
   (gap <= 0.001). Switching to adjusted R^2 as the criterion would wrongly
   signal data-driven selection.
